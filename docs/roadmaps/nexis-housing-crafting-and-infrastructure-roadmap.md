# Nexis Housing, Gated Crafting, and Infrastructure Roadmap

## Purpose
This roadmap defines the approved direction for hard-gated vehicle crafting, material-based housing progression, and infrastructure upgrades in Nexis.

This document extends the previously approved roadmaps for city vendors, items, and travel logistics.

---

## Core Direction
Crafting in Nexis is not intended to be easy, casual, or universally available.

This is now a hard rule.

### Approved crafting philosophy
- crafting must be skill-gated
- crafting must require real materials
- crafting should be hard
- vehicles should not be freely craftable by anyone
- housing and infrastructure upgrades must consume actual materials
- higher-end buildings and upgrades should feel like real progression milestones

This system should reward preparation, specialization, logistics, and long-term investment.

---

## Vehicle Crafting Rule
To craft a vehicle, a player must have:

- the required skills
- the required materials
- access to the required tools or facilities later if implemented

If any of these are missing, the player should not be able to craft the vehicle.

This is a hard gate, not a soft encouragement.

### Vehicle crafting is intended to be hard
Vehicles are significant progression assets and must not be treated like low-effort recipes.

They should require:
- multiple materials
- time investment
- relevant craftsmanship skills
- possibly multiple support skills depending on the vehicle type

Examples:
- cart: woodworking, basic construction, fittings
- wagon: stronger framework, wheels, axle work, leather harnessing, metal reinforcement
- rowboat: wood shaping, sealing, balance, fittings
- advanced vehicles later: multiple disciplines and higher skill thresholds

---

## Required Skill Philosophy
Vehicle crafting should require actual skill ownership, not just money.

### Example skill families
Depending on the project, crafting may require one or more of:
- woodworking
- smithing
- leatherworking
- engineering / tinkering
- maritime construction later
- logistics or transport support later

This should eventually be reflected in the relevant Nexis education or skill systems.

---

## Material Requirement Rule
Vehicles and structural upgrades must require real materials.

This is now approved as a core economy rule.

Players should need to source, gather, buy, transport, or trade for required materials.

Examples:
- wood
- metal fittings
- leather straps
- iron rims
- nails / fasteners
- treated cloth
- rope
- specialty materials for advanced variants

This ensures transport crafting ties back into the wider economy and city vendor structure.

---

## Cost and Difficulty Philosophy
Crafting should never be a cheaper bypass by default.

### Intended feel
- players craft because they invested in the right skills
- players craft because they secured the right materials
- players craft because they want control, upgrades, or specialization
- crafting without preparation should feel unrealistic or impossible

### Important rule
A player without the required skills should not be allowed to craft the vehicle just by paying money.

A player with the required skills should gain:
- meaningful cost reduction
- better access to self-made progression
- eventual access to higher-quality outputs or better upgrade efficiency

---

## Upgrade Rule for Vehicles
Vehicle upgrades should remain expensive even when they are craftable.

Skilled players reduce cost and improve access, but upgraded transport should still feel valuable and difficult to acquire.

### Upgrade examples
- reinforced frame
- larger cargo bay
- hidden compartment
- lighter wheels
- armored shell
- weatherproof covering
- better draft harness
- route-specialized adaptation

These should also require actual materials and relevant skills.

---

## Housing Expansion Direction
The player should have access to more housing tiers and more upgrade paths.

This is approved.

However, housing is not to be treated as a simple purchase ladder alone.

Higher houses and major upgrades should require actual materials to build or improve.

---

## Housing Philosophy
Housing should become a real infrastructure system, not just a list of passive buffs.

### Houses should provide
- storage expansion
- more upgrade potential
- better utility bonuses
- support for future staff, crafting, research, or recovery systems
- visual and economic progression

### Higher-tier housing should require
- real materials
- money
- time or staged progression later
- in some cases skill or contractor access later

This makes houses feel built, not magically purchased into existence.

---

## Housing Tier Direction
Housing should expand beyond the earlier basic structure.

### Example progression
- shack
- cottage
- townhouse
- manor
- estate
- advanced or specialized properties later

Each higher tier should allow:
- more storage
- more utility slots
- more upgrade categories
- stronger passive benefits
- more room for linked systems later

---

## Housing Upgrade Categories
Housing should support modular upgrades instead of only linear replacement.

### Structural upgrades
- reinforced walls
- expanded room count
- cellar / basement
- stable
- workshop bay
- storage annex

### Utility upgrades
- better bedding / comfort
- kitchen improvements
- water collection
- heat / weatherproofing
- lighting
- magical wards later

### Economic upgrades
- extra storage racks
- trade shelves
- cargo depot space
- animal stall capacity
- workshop efficiency

### Defensive upgrades
- locks and reinforced doors
- hidden compartments
- shutters / barriers
- alarm systems later

### Specialized upgrades
- alchemy room
- smithing corner
- arcane study
- healing chamber
- transport shed
- tattoo or craft studio later

---

## Material-Based Housing Rule
Significant housing upgrades and higher property tiers must require real materials.

Examples of required material categories:
- timber
- stone
- leather
- cloth
- iron fittings
- nails / fasteners
- glass later
- rarer components for elite upgrades later

This creates a healthy connection between:
- vendors
- materials
- transport
- storage
- trade
- crafting
- progression

---

## Why This Matters
This design creates strong system interdependence.

### Example progression loop
- player wants better wagon
- wagon requires materials and crafting skill
- player needs transport or trade to move materials efficiently
- better transport improves trade
- trade funds better house
- better house supports more storage and future crafting potential

This creates a proper growth loop rather than isolated mechanics.

---

## Future Support Systems
These systems can later connect to:
- player workshops
- guild or consortium construction
- city district reputation
- contractor NPCs
- property-based crafting bonuses
- material market fluctuations

These are future-facing hooks and not required for the first implementation pass.

---

## Balance Rules
- Crafting must never bypass progression completely.
- Vehicle crafting must be hard-gated by skill and material requirements.
- Housing upgrades must not be pure gold sinks; they should consume materials too.
- Higher-tier houses should feel earned and substantial.
- Skilled crafting should be significantly cheaper, but not trivial.
- Transport, housing, and materials should reinforce each other as systems.

---

## Implementation Roadmap

### Phase 1: Crafting Requirement Framework
Define:
- required skills per vehicle class
- required material categories
- hard gating logic
- upgrade material requirements

### Phase 2: Vehicle Recipe Structure

- cart
- wagon
- rowboat
- support upgrades

### Phase 3: Housing Tier Expansion
Define:
- expanded housing tiers
- storage values
- upgrade slot counts
- material requirements for major upgrades

### Phase 4: Housing Upgrade Matrix
Create modular upgrade categories and requirements for:
- structural
- utility
- economic
- defensive
- specialized upgrades

### Phase 5: System Integration
Tie together:
- transport
- materials
- trade
- vendors
- future crafting skills
- housing progression

### Phase 6: Advanced Infrastructure
Later additions may include:
- workshops
- district influence

- regional construction materials

---

## Hard Rules
- Vehicle crafting requires the proper skills.
- Vehicle crafting requires the proper materials.
- Vehicle crafting must be hard.
- Higher houses and major housing upgrades must use actual materials.
- Housing should offer more bonuses and more upgrade potential as tiers rise.
- Crafting and infrastructure should reinforce the wider economy, not bypass it.

---

## Working Summary
Nexis crafting and housing are now approved as hard-gated progression systems. Vehicles must require proper skills and materials to craft, and housing must expand into a material-based infrastructure system with deeper bonuses and modular upgrade potential. This is intended to make long-term progression feel earned, interconnected, and strategically meaningful.
